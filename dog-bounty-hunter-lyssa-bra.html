    DOG the Bounty Hunter's daughter Lyssa Chapman showed off her curves in a teeny sports bra and leggings.

    The 33-year-old - who recently celebrated Coming Out Day with her fiancee Leiana - displayed her slim figure in skimpy workout gear.

    Dog's daughter styled her hair in loose waves and wore minimal makeup for the sultry shot.

    The fitness enthusiast was her own best model as she wore designs from her clothing line, Baby.

    Lyssa captioned the glam photo: "Happy Hump Day. Sports bra and Leggings by @babybylyssachapman."

    The reality star's post comes after she shared a sweet tribute to her partner Leiana on Sunday.

    Sharing several smiling selfies with Leiana, Lyssa wrote: "Love is a beautiful thing!!

    "It should be celebrated , protected and cherished. If you’re in love... Congratulations!!!!"

    She added: "If you’re ashamed of your love, take your time, but know that when your ready, there is world full of open arms and warm embraces ready to welcome you !!"

    Lyssa then gave a shout out to her fiancée, writing: "Thanks for loving me baby @leiana13  #LoveisLove #nationalcomingoutday."

    In addition to the sweet selfie, she also posted a video of the two lounging on the beach during the "pretty" day.

    Back in August, she exclusively spoke to The Sun about how she and Leiana rekindled their relationship following a rough patch.

    Their relationship suffered as Lei's brother Gerrit died in a tragic plane crash in 2017 and Lyssa lost her stepmom, Beth Chapman, to cancer last year.

    Dog, whose real name is Duane Chapman, was married to Beth from 2006 until her death in 2019.

    While speaking to The Sun, Lyssa revealed that she considered moving away from the couple's Hawaii home to the US mainland earlier this year while she and Lei were going through a difficult time.

    The couple instead chose to have a "staycation," which allowed them to reconnect.

    She said: "I feel like before we got put on lockdown, things were a little bit rockier in the relationship, but it was almost a blessing for us to live together and not be able to leave.

    "It kind of forced us to spend a little bit more time together and rekindle something.

    "So when we were able to go to Kauai it was a chance just to be together and not have to worry about the kids and our schedules and work."

    Lyssa is one of Dog's 12 kids.
