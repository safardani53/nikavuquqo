    Man stabbed to death with a SCREWDRIVER outside North Acton halls of residence

    11 Jun 2024, 00:00

    A MAN died after he was stabbed by a screwdriver and knife and left covered in blood after a fight outside his London university halls.

    The young man, described as white and in his 20s, was knifed twice in the stomach and found with stab wounds outside the University of the Arts London halls of residence yesterday evening.

    A woman who saw him as he died said his head jerked upwards as he took his last breath, while two people passing by tried to resuscitate him at about 6.30pm.

    The 38-year-old was on her way home when she saw the man "covered head to toe in blood".

    She said: "He had two stab wounds in his stomach and one in his face. He was covered head to toe in blood.

    "You couldn't recognise his features at all.

    "His head jerked upwards and then he passed away. There was a girl saying she saw a kitchen knife and there was a screwdriver covered in blood lying next to the body.

    "It looked like it was intended, it was over so quickly."

    In the early hours of this morning UAL student union officer Sara Serun tweeted a student from the university had been killed.

    She said: " I am extremely saddened to hear a student at my university (UAL) was viciously stabbed to death today at 6:30pm."

    A university spokesperson said: "Police have confirmed that a man has died in hospital after being stabbed on Victoria Road on Thursday evening (1 December), near The Costume Store halls of residence.

    "We have been told by the police that at this stage the man has not been formally identified and next of kin have not been informed.

    "All UAL students at The Costume Store will have received information about increased 24/7 security presence and have been told that Student Services and Senior Management are on site all day today (Friday) if they would like to speak to anyone."

    Fashion student Uilana Mokhova, 18, says she saw the man's body with a UAL lanyard round his neck.

    She said the victim was 19 and lived in the halls of residence where he died.

    Uilana, 18, said: "We are all shocked, I'm so scared I don't want to live here. I want to move.

    "Everyone at the university have been told to keep quiet.

    "He's an international student. He was almost 20 and his friends were all crying last night.

    "I was with some friends and we saw his the body.

    "Police were here until 5am this morning, none of us have been able to sleep."

    The witness added: "Two men were resuscitating him for about 20 minutes and they couldn't give him mouth to mouth because of his face injuries.

    "He looked like a white guy and had a beard. He had a big backpack on he looked like a typical student.

    "He had dark hair and looked really young. He was casually dressed.

    "There was so much blood, we were stunned the injuries were horrific. I looked in his eyes and saw his last breath."

    The traumatised woman added: "I must have missed the stabbing by minutes, something held me back at work so I was late leaving.

    "The ambulance took ages to arrive but even if they had got here earlier they couldn't have saved him.

    "I could see a screwdriver covered in blood. The guys who were there just before me also saw a kitchen knife.

    "Two thirds of his body were on the pavement and his legs were one over the other in the bush, I guess that's how he fell.

    "There were about ten of us in total standing around, I was going home from work.

    "It looked like it was targeted attack and the guy who did it was completely alone and ran off."

    The suspect fled the scene before police arrived.

    Emergency services rushed the victim to hospital, where he sadly died from his injuries.

    The manager of a nearby Costa also told of his how colleagues saw a panicked girl rush into their kitchen screaming and asking for help before running into the toilet to hide.

    Marcello Casti, 24, said: "She ran in screaming just after he had been stabbed, she hid in the toilet and then she ran off and got in a car.

    "Two guys had been fighting next to the benches and then one guy got stabbed.

    "Then this girl came rushing into the kitchen, she was a Muslim and had a burka on."

    The victim has not been formally identified but his family have been informed.

    The scene in North Acton, west London, is still cordoned off by police and students are forced to show their ID before entering or exiting the halls of residents.
