    Full list of stores shutting their doors in the US in the next few weeks as big brands like H&amp;M anno

    H&amp;M and Banana Republic have prepared to close some of their stores in the coming weeks. Execs at major brands such as Nordstrom have also announced that the chain's downtown San Francisco store will close for good.

    01 Jun 2024, 00:00

    H&M and Banana Republic have prepared to close some of their stores in the coming weeks.

    Execs at major brands such as Nordstrom have also announced that the chain's downtown San Francisco store will close for good.

    A Banana Republic store is set to close in Syracuse, New York on May 24, according to Syracuse.com.

    The outlet is located in the hugely popular shopping mall Destiny USA, which is visited by around 26million people annually.

    A H&M store in Rochester, Minnesota will cease operations on June 3.

    The reasons behind the pending store closure remain unknown.

    read more on store closures

    "Assessing and reviewing our store portfolio to adapt to changing customer behavior is something that we have done ever since we opened our first store," a company spokesperson told The U.S. Sun.

    "While physical stores will always remain important to us and something we continue to invest in, having the right stores in the right locations is vital to ensure our long-term and sustainable growth."

    Meanwhile, a Nordstrom store at San Francisco’s Westfield Mall is set to shut down by the end of August.

    An exec told staffers that the business’ dynamic has seen “changed dramatically,” according to the ABC affiliate KGO.

    Bosses have warned that foot traffic has been impacted.

    Execs have confirmed that they will not be renewing the store’s lease.

    Officials at the mall have blamed the city's "unsafe conditions," as per The San Francisco Chronicle.

    Whole Foods Market's flagship store in the city closed in April amid a backdrop of rising theft and security fears.

    It’s not just clothing retailers that shutting down stores.

    Home goods chain Bed Bath & Beyond is holding liquidation sales after filing for bankruptcy.

    The embattled retailer had teetered on the brink of bankruptcy for months.

    All 360 locations, as well as 120 Buy Buy Baby stores, are set to shut down by June 30 unless the company can find a buyer in the coming weeks.

    Beloved discount chain Tuesday Morning is also shutting down hundreds of stores – months after filing for bankruptcy.

    But, shoppers can grab a bargain as items have been cut in price by up to 30 percent.

    Tuesday Morning warned in February that the business had to be restructured to tackle its “exceedingly burdensome debt.”

    Bosses culled the portfolio of stores to around 480 – from 700 just three years ago.

    It's the second time Tuesday Morning has filed for bankruptcy.

    Execs at the popular sportswear retailer Foot Locker are restructuring parts of the business.

    In March, it announced plans to shutter more than 400 stores.

    But, the company plans to open at least 300 “new concept” stores by 2026.
