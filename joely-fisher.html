    Joely Fisher’s Biography

    Joely Fisher was born on October 29, 1967, in Burbank, California, to entertainers Edwin “Eddie” Fisher (1928-2010) and Connie Stevens. Her paternal grandparents immigrated to the United States from the Russian Empire, and her mother was a Catholic with Italian, Irish, and Ashkenazi ancestry.

    Tricia Leigh Fisher is her younger sister, and her parents divorced in 1969, leaving the two girls to be raised by Stevens. Debbie Reynolds later married her father. Joely and her sister traveled the world with her father as a result of her father’s job, and they attended different schools and had tutors.

    She attended Emerson College in Boston after graduating from Beverly Hills High School. She later attended the University of Paris for one semester before attending an acting retreat in Italy during the summer of 1987.

    Career

    Joely received her first film role in the comedy Pretty Smart, starred her sister Tricia, in 1987. In 1991, she starred in the drama Dedicated to the One I Loved. She had a feature role in the film I’ll Do Anything in 1994, which helped her get bigger roles in other films. She also appeared as a guest star in several television shows, including Grace Under Fire, Coach, Growing Pains, Caroline in the City, The Golden Palace, and The Outer Limits.

    Fisher was cast as Paige Clark on the sitcom series Ellen in 1994, which proved to be a major career breakthrough for her. She portrayed the character from 1994 to 1998. She also performed the series’ theme song with Clea Lewis. She had a role in the 1999 film Inspector Gadget, which came out immediately after the series ended.

    Joely landed a starring role in the drama series Wild Card from 2003 to 2005, playing Zoe Busiek. This was followed by a guest appearance on Desperate Housewives. From 2006 to 2010, she co-starred with Brad Garret in the Fox TV series ‘Til Death.

    Fisher rose to prominence as a singer, landing roles on Broadway and appearing on a number of albums. She made her Broadway debut in 1994 as a replacement in the revival of Grease. In 1998, she made another appearance in the revival of Cabaret.

    Her most well-known album was “Tradition; a Family at Christmas.” Fisher and her mother Connie sang a variety of Christmas carols on the album. Other albums by her include Welcome to Holiday Inn, One for My Baby, and Come Back to Me.
