    Bristol Palin took to her blog on Wednesday, Oct. 22, to share her side of the infamous birthday party brawl that took place at an Anchorage birthday party last month.

    “Most of the time, I let the lies pass,” wrote Bristol, 24. “After all, the truth eventually comes out. But when people are selling ‘scoop,’ photos, and false information…sometimes the truth just isn’t as profitable… That’s why, after a month and a half of hearing rumors about myself and family, I’ve finally decided to comment about the situation. Instead of listening to all the people who weren’t there—people who claim they heard this from their cousin/brother/sister-in-law/step-daughter/long lost little brother–let me tell you what actually happened.”

    Palin’s perspective on the incident included some disparities from party host Korey Klingenmeyer‘s point of view in the police report released Oct. 9. “Our friend got knocked out from a cheap shot from behind. (His injury resulted in ten stitches, so it was really low),” she wrote. (In the police report, Bristol’s father Todd Palin said that the friend, Steven, was attacked and knocked to the ground, making it “a situation they couldn’t walk away from.”)

    “When Willow saw all this happening she looked at the guy’s mom and said ‘get ahold of your son,'” Bristol wrote. “But apparently the apple didn’t fall too far from the tree, because his mom pushed Willow. A grown woman pushed my little sister.”

    By then, Bristol was already in her family’s limo parked in the driveway of Klingenmeyer’s home. “But when Willow ran to me crying, telling me that some lady had pushed her down, I got out of the car to go talk to her. Any big sister would do this,” she recalled, adding that “next to God, family is the most important thing to me.”

    What happened after was even more terrifying for the Dancing With the Stars alum, who claimed Klingenmeyer physically accosted her in his pursuit of fame. “After I got out of the car, I didn’t get far,” the eldest Palin daughter revealed. “I never even got to talk to his mom, because a guy in his late thirties or early forties got in my face. He was towering over me—probably at 6 foot something and over 200 pounds. He puffed his chest out and started yelling. ‘You c—!’ he looked right in my eyes and said. ‘Get the f— out of here, you slut!’ I was alarmed that things had gotten so bad so fast. But it got even worse when this guy started pushing me. He had his hands on me, pushing me down. That’s when I swung and hit his face.”

    In the police report, Klingenmeyer claimed that Bristol had punched him five or six times after that initial hit. “He says she gets up and tries to punch him again and he grabs her fist again and pushes her away and she falls down again,” the report read. Bristol, though, said that initial punch was the only one she threw.

    “It was scary and awful. He held me down until someone got me out of the situation. That’s it – that’s the story,” she wrote. “I didn’t ‘swing and hit him seven times with a strong right hook’ as so many so-called news stories have reported. After this incident, I still had a perfect manicure on all ten of my nails.”

    According to the police report, there was a “strong odor of alcohol coming from the group” when officers John Daily and Justin Blake arrived on the scene. Bristol’s 25-year-old brother Track “appeared heavily intoxicated and acted belligerent at first” as he was without his shirt and bloodied “around his mouth and on his hands,” the report read. Former GOP Vice Presidential candidate Sarah and Todd Palin both “appeared upset and in a verbal argument with other individuals at the scene,” while Willow was described as “extremely agitated.”
