    He was a solid contributor for the Phoenix Suns of the NBA but was waived by the team on February 1, 2023, after a season.

    Duane signed the New York Knicks in a two-way contract in 2023, after which Washington divided his playing time with Westchester of the NBA G League.

    At the end of the 2022-2023 season, Washington scored a career-high 26 points, four rebounds, and eight assists on December 27, 2022, in a win over the Memphis Grizzlies.

    The Ohio State alumnus is the son of former NBA player Duane Washington who last played in the league for the Los Angeles Clippers during the 1992-93 season.

    Early Life & Education

    Duane Washington Jr. was born Duane Eddy Washington Jr. on March 24, 2000, in Frankfurt, Germany.

    He comes from a lineage of basketball players. Washington is the son of former professional basketball player Duane Washington Sr. and Therese Washington.

    His father played for the Skyliners Frankfurt in Germany, after which he did not play for any other team.

    Along with his father, his uncle Derek Fisher also played in the NBA for 18 seasons until hanging his cleats in 2014.

    DWJ was raised in Grand Rapids, Michigan, where he attended Grand Rapids Christian High School for three years and then transferred to Sierra Canyon School in Los Angeles.

    DWJ later transferred to his uncle Fisher’s basketball camp to fulfill his NBA dreams and, during that time, was considered a three-star recruit by Rivals and a four-star by ESPN.

    Consequently, he received athletic scholarships from UCLA, Butler, and Michigan, but he chose Ohio State and committed to play on September 20, 2017.

    After playing for the Ohio State Buckeyes from 2018 to 2021, he declared for the 2021 NBA Draft while maintaining his college eligibility initially.

    Nevertheless, he announced his intention to remain in the draft on June 29, 2021.

    Undrafted In the 2021 NBA Draft

    Despite his outstanding collegiate career, he went undrafted in the 2021 NBA Draft, joining notable fellow undrafted players like Austin Reaves, Joel Ayayi & Aaron Henry.

    Nevertheless, Indiana Pacers signed Washington on a two-way contract on August 5, 2021, during which he also played with the Fort Wayne Mad Ants, the Pacer’s G-League affiliate.

    His two-way contract with the team was converted into a standard one on April 7, 2022. Nonetheless, the Pacers waived Washington Jr. on July 14, 2022.

    While with the Pacers, Duane Washington Jr. made a franchise-high seven three-pointers in a loss against the Pelicans (117-113) on January 24, 2022.

    It broke the previous record of six three-pointers, each by Chuck Person and Chris Duarte.

    Then, Washington was signed by the Phoenix Suns on August 3, 2022, on a two-way contract.

    His term with the team ended on February 1, 2023, making him a free agent when the Suns signed the Vanderbilt Commodores alumnus Saban Lee on a two-way contract.

    With the Suns, the NBA player scored his career-high 26 points in a 125-108 victory against the Grizzlies on December 27, 2022.

    Father Washington Sr. & Uncle Are Former NBA Players

    As mentioned, Duane Washington Jr.’s father, Duane Washington Sr., played two seasons in the NBA for New Jersey Nets (1988) & Los Angeles Clippers (1993.)

    Similarly, he played professionally overseas in Spain, France, Colombia, Venezuela, Germany, and Israel. Washington Sr. is famous for his professional career, as a father of an NBA player, and for his legal issues.

    On September 18, 2012, Washington Sr. hit a pedestrian walking alongside I-96 in Michigan and left the accident scene.

    After nine months, he accepted a plea and was sentenced to 60 days in jail, 100 hours of community service, and 18 months probation.

    Regarding DWJ’s uncle, Derek Fisher played in the NBA for 18 seasons, spent most of his career with the Lakers, and won 5 NBA championships. After retiring from basketball, he began his coaching career.

    He was announced as the head coach for the Los Angeles Sparks of the WNBA on December 5, 2018, and in 2020 was appointed as general manager.

    After two years, Derek parted with the Sparks as a head coach and general manager in 2022. It is unknown if he is still in the coaching profession or has left it.
