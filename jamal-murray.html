    Jamal Murray | Basketball Career 

    Jamal merits much of his success to his father, Roger Murray, who helped and guided him toward his dream. Below mentioned is the breakdown of his journey in basketball:

    High-School career

    For his high school, Murray had firstly joined Grand River Collegiate Institute in his hometown, Kitchener. Later, he transferred to Orangeville Prep School in Orangeville, Ontario.

    There, his father served as the assistant coach, and he became a member of the school’s team. He joined hands with Thon Marker to form a duo and defeat many American schools and bring them victory.

    Later, he played the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) basketball.

    College Career

    Jamal’s college life began on 24th June 2015. he committed to Kentucky to play under the leadership of Coach John Calipari.

    Despite being a freshman, he was featured on the Midseason Top 25 list for the John R Wooden Award. For the Naismith Trophy, he was also named to the 35-man midseason watchlist.

    He appeared in 36 games during his first year and averaged 20.0 points, with 5.2 rebounds and 2.2 assists while shooting 40.8% from the three-point range.

    Later, after his freshman season, he was named a third-team All-American by the Associated Press.

    Similarly, his effort and hard work led him to be a member of the All-SEC first team and the SEC All-Freshman Team.

    One of Murray’s most historical moments is his 20.0 points per game by any freshman in Kentucky’s program history.

    Finally, in April 2016, he was declared for the NBA draft, which led him to leave his final three years of college eligibility.
