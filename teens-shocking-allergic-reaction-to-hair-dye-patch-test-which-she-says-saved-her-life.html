    Allergic reaction to hair dye patch test saves teen's life in Wales

    A TEEN girl is lucky to be alive after a routine allergy test left her with a full body rash, unable to move her neck and weeping EARS. Doctors told Sophie Warren she would have DIED if the dye had been put all over.

    05 Mar 2024, 00:00

    A TEEN girl is lucky to be alive after a routine allergy test left her with a full body rash, unable to move her neck and weeping EARS.

    Doctors told Sophie Warren she would have DIED if the dye had been put all over.

    Hairdressers advised the 16-year-old to have a patch test behind her ears before having the colour all over - even though she had dyed her hair before with no problems.

    Just an hour after the dye was tested, her ears ballooned and started weeping.

    She immediately wiped the colour off - but the next day the reaction was even worse, and the teen was left unable to move her neck.

    related stories

    Sophie, from Troedyrhiw, Wales, said: “I'd tried to dye my hair but it had just gone yellow, so I went to the hairdressers to see if they could fix it.

    "They told me I needed to have a routine patch test, and they put a semi-permanent behind my left ear and a permanent behind my right ear and booked my appointment for two days later.

    "I left the salon and got on the train to Cardiff, and during that hour-long journey my ears just blew up.

    "I went to a toilet in the train station and rubbed the dye straight off, thinking my skin was just being sensitive.

    "But when I woke up the next morning, my pillows were soaked from where my ears had been weeping.

    "It had just got worse."

    But even though she'd never had a reaction like this before, Sophie brushed it off as sensitive skin and thought her ears would go down.

    It wasn't until she went to see stylists at Hairport Hair, in Merthyr Tydfil 48 hours after the patch test - that they told her to go to the hospital

    The student - who can never colour her hair again - said: "The hairdresser told me to go to the hospital straight away.

    "I was seen after 15 minutes and the doctor told me if the dye had been put all over my head it would have killed me.

    "I was just in shock."

    Sophie was prescribed antibiotics which reduced the swelling, but on Tuesday morning she woke up and was covered in a rash.

    She said: "I went to the doctors and they said the infection that was in my ear had spread to my throat, neck and body.

    "At its worst I couldn't turn my head without moving my whole body."

    Sophie had experimented with colour in the past and even had blonde highlights - but warns others to always get a patch test in advance.

    The teen said: "I'm so glad the hairdressers made me have a patch test, they've been so good.

    "I can never colour my hair again, but I don't even want to touch another colour in my life.

    "I'm lucky to be alive."

    Paul Reardon, managing director at Hairport Hair, said: "We skin test everybody.

    "It can be dangerous – we must take it seriously."
