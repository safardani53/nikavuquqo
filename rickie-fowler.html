    Early Life, Family, & Education

    Rick Yutaka Fowler, famous as Rickie Fowler, was born in Murrieta, California, on December 13, 1988, to Rod Fowler (father) and Lynn Fowler (mother).

    He got his middle name, “Yutaka,” from his maternal grandfather, from Japan, while his maternal grandmother was Navajo Native American.

    Rickie has always followed the Japanese culture and is proud of his heritage. Talking about his education, he was born and raised in Murrieta and went to Murrieta High School.

    Rickie’s father owned a trucking company.

    Similarly, his sister also participated in bike sports.

    Even Rickie followed his father footsteps to be a dirt biker, but later, he left riding due to an accident. So, instead, his grandfather introduced him to golf.

    After that, Rickie started playing golf, and it won’t be a mistake to say that he learned everything independently while he learned golf by playing on the driving porch.

    Later, in 2005 summer, Rickie won the Western Junior, and again in 2007, he helped the U.S. win the Walker Cup.

    In 2007, during his senior year at high school, he won the SW League Final and helped his team win the state final.

    The following year Fowler won the Sunnehanna Amature title and again defended the same title in 2008.

    Similarly, in 2009 Fowler won all four matches and finished third in Sunnehanna Amature while helping the U.S. defend the Walker Cup. He also received the 2008 Ben Hogan Award.

    Professional Career

    Rickie Fowler finished as runner-up in the Nationwide Children’s Hospital Invitational.

    2010-2013

    At the Waste Management Phoenix Open, held in February of 2010, Fowler finished second with a score of 15-under-par.

    Likewise, in Rickie’s third PGA Tour, he entered the final round and became a runner-up at the Memorial Tournament in Dublin, Ohio.

    This was his first PGA victory, eventually taking Rickie into the top 50 of the Official World Golf Ranking.

    In September 2010, U.S. Ryder Cup chose Rickie as a captain’s pick for their team. 

    Rickie was only 21 when he made his U.S. Ryder Cup debut, becoming the youngest Ryder Cup player of all time.

    Further, in 2011, Fowler tied for 5th in The Open Championship in Royal St Gorge.

    After a disappointing performance in the FedEx Cup last round, Fowler stood at 37 in the FedEx Cup points.

    However, he enhanced his playing level and earned a $132,000 bonus in the FedEx Cup. His first professional win was at OneAsia Tour’s Kolon Korea Open.

    In 2011, Fowler was ranked the 32nd best golf player in the world.

    Fowler defeated Rory McIlroy and D.A. Points, which marked his first PGA Tour victory.  

    Likewise, in 2013 Fowler finished as a runner-up in the Australian PGA Championship, four shots behind the tournament.

    Fowler stood third even after holding the lead for most of the day.

    He was placed 8th after the Tour Championship. Not so surprisingly, he was ranked the 10th best golf player in the world.

    Fowler won the Honda Classic for his fourth PGA Tour on February 26 and preserved his 54-hole lead to win for the first time in his career.
