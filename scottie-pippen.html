    Fans of the NBA need no introduction to Scottie Pippen. Scotty Maurice Pippen is a former American basketball player. He is the perfect example of the proverb “talent has no boundaries.”

    However, fame and success did not come easy to Scottie Pippen. This NBA legend had to prove himself before earning a spot on the basketball team.

    Likewise, his behind the scene is much more different than his highlighted reel. Pippen started as an equipment manager at the college.

    Neither was he offered a scholarship in the college. A six-time NBA champion was seen without “NBA potential” by his coach.

    Moreover, Scottie is the only NBA player who won the NBA title and Olympic Gold medal in the same year, twice in the years 1992 and 1996.

    So what makes Scottie so different from the rest of the players? Before digging a little deeper, let’s have a look at quick facts.

    Scottie Pippen | Childhood and Education

    Life isn’t easy all the time, neither was it for Scottie Pippen. Scottie was born to Ethel Pippen and Preston Pippen in Hamburg, Arkansas, on September 25, 1965.

    He is the youngest child of their 12 children. Scottie did not grow up rich and did not have a lavish home as he has now.

    Instead, the Pippens grew up in a two-room house in a small town with 3,500 people. His father worked in a paper mill factory, and they were struggling financially.

    Scottie’s parents couldn’t afford to send his children to universities. Then, during his freshman year, things got much more difficult when his father suffered a stroke.

    He had no other option but to leave his job as he was placed in a wheelchair until his death.

    Shortly after this tragedy, his older brother Ronnie was also placed in a wheelchair following an accident.

    Scottie’s only shot in attending a college was through a scholarship. He attended Hamburg High School and dominated his school basketball team.

    Likewise, he played as a point guard and led his team to the state playoffs, yet colleges weren’t interested in Scottie’s scholarships.

    Scottie Pippen | College Career

    After not being offered any scholarship, his high school coach called to favor his fellow friend from the University of Central Arkansas.

    He was offered the Basic Education Opportunity Grant. With not many options, he got enrolled in the University of Central Arkansas.

    Initially, he was the team’s equipment manager as the coach felt he did not have the upper body strength.

    It was only when players left the team; he grabbed the opportunity for the open spots.

    Surprisingly, Pippen grew from 6’1″ to 6’6″ before his sophomore year and developed upper body strength.

    With his new strength and experiences as the point guard, he became the best player during his sophomore year. Not only did his average point grew, but also his growth spurt shot to 6’8″.

    In those four years of college, he played 93 games with an average of 17.2 per game.

    Scottie Pippen | Professional Career

    Chicago Bulls 

    Pippen was drafted in 1987 with the Seattle SuperSonics and was later traded with Chicago Bulls. When he joined the Bulls, he was overshadowed by Michael Jordan. Jordan was already an All-Star.

    Following the mentorship of Jordan and his coach Phil Jackson, he further polished his existing skills and developed a new one.

    Finally, in 1990, Pippen made his debut in NBA-All Star. After Jordan’s first abrupt retirement in 1993-1994, Pippen led the Bulls to win 55 games.

    He proved to the world that he is more than a sidekick and finally drew people’s attention as deserved.

    Similarly, he earned the Most Valuable Awards and the first of three straight All-Nba First Team.

    For Dream Team & Houston Rockets 

    Pippen was one of the “Dream Team members,” which was the United States men’s Olympic basketball team in 1992.

    It was the first American Olympic team that featured active professional players from the NBA.

    After spending 11 seasons with the Bulls, he was traded to Houston Rockets for Roy Royers. His contract with the rockets lasted for 12 seasons.

    After that, he joined his former Olympic teammate Charles Barkley and Hakeem Olajuwon.

    During his time with the Rockets, his average score was 14.5 per game, the lowest since his rookie year.

    Portland Trail Blazers

    Scottie joined Portland Trail Blazers for the 1999-2000 NBA season.

    While competing against the Bulls in the 1999-2000 season, he was honored with a video tribute showing his 11-year highlights, which Scottie described as “emotional.”

    After winning Western Conference Finals, they were defatted by Los Angeles Lakers, which later won the season.

    He played with the Blazers for the 2000-01 season as well and played 64 games. Unfortunately, he missed 18 games due to his injury.

    Furthermore, he stayed with the Portland Blazers for two more seasons.

    Chicago Bulls 

    After playing for Portland for four seasons, Pippen rejoined the Bulls. He signed the contract for two years worth $10 million.

    Although being an expert and a guide to new team members, he was injured numerous times.

    In 2004, the team failed to qualify for the playoffs, which was the first time in his career. In 2005, he announced his retirement due to his injuries.

    For the 2003-04 season, Scottie played 23 games. His jersey number 33 was retired by the Bulls, making him the fourth player to hang from the rafters.

    As of 2019-2020, Scottie ranks number 2 for most minutes played and most points scored.

    By the end of his time with the Bulls, Pippen’s playoff stats averaged 18.1 points, 7.7 rebounds, and 5.2 assists. 

    After Retirement

    Although he retired in 2004, he played his last professional game with Torpan Pojat and Sundsvall Dragons in 2008.

    Then, at the age of 42, he made a comeback to compete in Finnish League. The Dragons had paid him $66,000 for the games.

    In 2010, Pippen was the Bulls team ambassador and, in 2012, was named a senior advisor to the President and COO of the Bulls.

    After his retirement, he has made appearances in many TV shows movies such as Midgetsvs. Mascots, Fresh off the Boat, Chicago Fire, and The Last Dance.

    Who is Scottie Pippen’s wife? Spouse(s) and Children

    Pippen was first married to his then-girlfriend Karen McCollum in July 1988. They already had a son Antron before their marriage.

    The couple married a year later Antron’s birth. They split up after two years. 

    In 1993, Scottie was dating a model Sonya Roby who later gave birth to Tyler and Taylor’s twins. Scottie initially denied the responsibility, but the DNA test proved otherwise.

    But unfortunately, Tyler died nine days later after his birth. After ending his relation with Roby, he dated and was later engaged to Yvette De Leon.

    The couple together gave birth to a girl, Sierra. The couple broke up following a domestic violence incident.

    Yvette had to fight for her daughter in court, demanding him to support Sierra financially. Scottie eventually lost the case and settled it by paying a considerable sum. 

    Scottie dated and later married Real Housewives of Miami star Larsa Younan in 1995. She was 22 years old at the time of their marriage and nine years younger than Scottie.

    The couple gave birth to four children Scotty Jr, Preston, Justin, and Sophia, in 2000, 2002, 2005, and 2008. 

    After 21 years of marriage, the couple announced they were filing for divorce in 2018 due to “irreconcilable difference.”
