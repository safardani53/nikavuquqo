    HGTV'S Fixer to Fabulous first premiered in 2019 and introduced fans to home renovators Dave and Jenny Marrs.

    Fans were also introduced to carpenter Chase Looney and now they want to know what happened to his eye.

    What is Fixer to Fabulous?

    Fixer to Fabulous is a popular home improvement show on HGTV.

    The series followers Dave and Jenny as they turn "timeworn homes into contemporary living spaces" in the hometown of Bentonville, Arkansas.

    Over the years, they have completed close to 300 renovations. They also run Marrs Developing, a "company that specializes in the renovation and restoration of historic homes in the Northwest Arkansas area."

    The show can be watched on HGTV or streamed on Discovery+.

    Who is Chase Looney?

    Looney is arguably best known for his career as an HGTV personality.

    Prior to his role on HGTV, he worked as a firefighter.

    What some people might not know is that Looney and the Marrs' were actually friends before the show started, which made his involvement even more special.

    “He wasn’t really given an option NOT to be with us on the show, but he graciously came along for the ride and we couldn’t imagine not having him with us,” Jenny wrote on Facebook, via Yahoo! Entertainment.

    Most read in Entertainment

    At this time, additional details on his personal life are unclear because he often stayed out of the spotlight.

    What happened to Chase Looney on Fixer to Fabulous?

    When Looney first premiered on the show, he had both eyes, but after the pilot episode was filmed, he was involved in a serious accident that caused injury to his face.

    While setting up a firework display for the Fourth of July, a tube exploded in his face.

    He was then rushed to the hospital and had to undergo an eight-hour surgery, but doctors were unable to save his eye, according to Yahoo! Entertainment.

    "The following days in the hospital, I was given the news that I lost my eye. That it would have to be removed. That I might not have movement in the left side of my face and that the scarring would be permanent," Looney previously explained in a blog post.

    During the whole ordeal, Dave and Jenny were right by his side. At first, he didn't think he could return to TV, but the celebrity couple convinced him otherwise.

    "I told them, 'I don’t think I have the face for TV anymore.' Through tears and laughter, they reassured me I never had a face for TV anyway,” Looney joked.

    After recovering, he returned to Fixer to Fabulous rocking an eye patch.
