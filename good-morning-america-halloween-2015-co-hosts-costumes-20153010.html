    Good Morning America Halloween 2015: See the Co-Hosts' Costumes!

    Need some last-minute Halloween inspiration? The Good Morning America co-hosts have you covered! The ABC crew debuted a series of pop culture-related costumes on Friday, Oct. 30, giving the Today shows Peanuts-themed gang a run for their money.

    04 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Need some last-minute Halloween inspiration? The Good Morning America co-hosts have you covered! The ABC crew debuted a series of pop culture-related costumes on Friday, Oct. 30, giving the Today show’s Peanuts-themed gang a run for their money. 

    Look who it is: @sarahaines and @NickLachey as Barbie and Ken! #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/2vshyXDBVM

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    Looking picture-perfect, Sara Haines and Nick Lachey came out in pink boxes dressed like plastic couple Barbie and Ken. But they weren’t the only two high-profile twosome. 

    Robin Roberts and T.J. Holmes looked fierce as Cookie and Lucious Lyon from Fox’s hit musical drama Empire. Roberts wore a purple fur stole while Holmes rocked Lucious’ navy suit and signature decorative scarf. 

    It’s @taylorswift13 time as @arobach does #BadBlood! #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/Gl0c0p5CvJ

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    Rolling in with her own (attached) cardboard cutouts of the “Bad Blood” squad, Amy Robach dressed like superstar Taylor Swift from her single’s hit, action-packed music video. And George Stephanopoulos donned a ginger wig to be Swift’s real-life bestie Ed Sheeran.

    Doing “Thinking Out Loud?” @GStephanopoulos as @edsheeran of course! #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/o7zc3YewT9

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    Going along with the musical theme, Paula Faris wore Katy Perry’s pinwheel Super Bowl look and even brought along both Left and Right Shark (Ron Claiborne and Rob Marciano).

    #LeftShark alert! @paulafaris, @RobMarciano and @RonEClaiborne as @katyperry, her Super Bowl sharks! #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/CPMMzWctEF

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    Lara Spencer’s Donald Trump costume was “huuuuuge!” as she posed at a Trump podium and wore a wig identical to the presidential hopeful’s infamous comb-over. 

    Pregnant meteorologist Ginger Zee game out ready to dominate as the bright green Hulk, bulging biceps and all! And correspondent Dan Harris dressed up as “the Chosen One” aka Harry Potter.

    We have a wizard on set… @danbharris as Harry Potter! #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/kSRwC78yEg

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    Guests Rumer Willis and Val Chmerkovskiy also got in on the fun, dressing as Magenta from Rocky Horror Picture Show and a devil, respectively. 

    But these costumes weren’t the only ones the co-hosts sported for the spooky holiday. Robach, Zee, Spencer, Jesse Palmer, and Holmes also did a spoof of the upcoming Star Wars film, titled, “Star Wars: The Lost Episode,” dressing as some of the franchise’s iconic characters. 

    Star Wars: The Lost Episode @arobach as Yoda @Ginger_Zee as Rey & Leia @LaraSpencer as Mrs. Chewbacca #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/hCl1uj0x4S

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015

    And to properly outfit her baby bump, Zee also gave her Twitter followers the chance to see her creative maternity looks. She wore an avocado costume, dressed as the Pillsbury Doughboy, and decorated her bare stomach to look like a Mr. Potato Head doll. 

    Oh my… @Ginger_Zee‘s costumes for her and her future baby are just amazing. #GMAHalloween pic.twitter.com/plfr5rmoOz

    — Good Morning America (@GMA) October 30, 2015
