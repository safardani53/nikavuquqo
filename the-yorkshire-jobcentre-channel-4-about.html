    CHANNEL 4's new docu-series The Yorkshire Jobcentre delves inside the bleak world of job centres.

    It follows the work of jobcentre staff in Leeds, as they deal with the needs of their clients whilst also navigating their own lives.

    What time is The Yorkshire Jobcentre on Channel 4 tonight?

    The Yorkshire Jobcentre continues tonight (August 24, 2020) on Channel 4 at 9pm.

    Episodes of The Yorkshire Jobcentre are all one hour long.

    Producers spent more than five years working on the project, talking to the Department for Work and Pensions to secure access to Southern House.

    Each episode will be available to watch after they have aired on 4oD.

    What is The Yorkshire Jobcentre about?

    The Yorkshire Jobcentre is a docu-series that gives viewers an inside peak into Southern House – a bustling Jobcentre Plus in the heart of Leeds.

    The official synopsis reads: "This series meets the dedicated job coaches and their customers at one of Yorkshire's busiest Jobcentres."

    With 35 job coaches seeing up to 600 customers every 24 hours, anyone and everyone can come through the doors.

    Channel 4’s Commissioning Editor Rita Daniels said: “Lots of series have been made about benefits, mostly focusing on the extreme end of the spectrum and the long-term unemployed.

    “This will be the first series placing equal emphasis on the human stories on both sides of the desk – challenging preconceptions about staff and claimants. And, as the last government agency on the high street, there couldn’t be a more pertinent precinct to speak to life in the UK today.”

    Filming was first announced in September 2019.

    How many episodes of The Yorkshire Jobcentre are there?

    Yorkshire Job Centre consists of six one-hour-long episodes.

    The first episode sees 61-year-old former account worker Karen, who is struggling to compete in today’s job market today.

    Meanwhile, Kenny, 32, is seen by work coach Bernie to try to get him back on his feet.
