    Details On Parents & Siblings

    Jarrett Culver, son of Hiawatha Culver Jr and Regina Culver, was born on February 20, 1999, in Dallas, Texas.

    His father is a Pastor and former Texas Tech team chaplain.

    Thanks to Culver’s father who serves as the pastor of Rising Star Baptist Church in Lubbock and the team chaplain for Texas Tech, the family’s religious foundation is just as strong.

    Talking about his siblings, he has two older brothers, and both of them are quite athletic and are involved in sports.

    Trey Culver (b. July 18, 1996), the eldest of all, is a track & field athlete.

    He tied for the fourth-best indoor leap in NCAA history in 2018 while competing for Texas Tech as a two-time NCAA indoor high jump champion in 2016 and 2017.

    Jarrett Culver’s middle brother J.J. Culver (b. December 14, 1997), is a basketball player who played for the Wayland Baptist University. 

    After three years at Wayland, J.J. signed with Lithunian-basketball club BC Mazeikiai in 2021 before trade to another Lithunian club BC Šilutė.

    For the 2022-23 season, he signed with German Pro B team Rasta Vechta II before promotion to Pro A’s Rasta Vechta.

    High School & College Basketball

    Jarrett completed high school at Coronado High School in Lubbock,Texas,  where he was trained under head coach Randy Dean.

    A three-star prospect, Culver decided to enroll at Texas Tech University despite receiving numerous offers from universities like University of Texas, Baylor, and the University of Illinois.

    He averaged 11.2 points, 4.8 rebounds, and 1.1 steals per game as a rookie in his college career and, in a conference play, scored 229 points breaking the freshmen record set by Martin Zeno, which was 223 points in 2005.

    Jarrett was also named Big 12 Player of the Year after leading the Red Raiders at the end of a conference play.

    After his sophomore year, he declared for the NBA draft, concluding the year with an average of 18.5 points, 6.4 rebounds & 3.7 assists per game.

    Jarrett Culver’s NBA Journey & Achievements

    After his sophomore year at college, Jarrett went for NBA draft & became the sixth overall pick of Phoenix Suns in the 2019 NBA Draft.

    However, his draft rights was traded to Minnesota Timberwolves immediately in exchange for Dario Šarić and the 11th overall pick (Cameron Johnson).

    With the Timberwolves, Culver made his NBA debut on October 23, 2019, in a 127-126 overtime win over the Nets, scoring four points, two assists, and one steal.

    Then, on January 18, 2020, he scored his rookie-year high & also his career-high 26 points in a loss (112-122) against Toronto Raptors.

    After playing two seasons for the Timberwolves, he was traded to Memphis Grizzlies on August 25, 2021, with Juancho Hernangómez in exchange for Patrick Beverley.

    With the Grizzlies, he scored his then season-high 14 points (with 2 rebounds & 1 assist) in a 110-139 loss against the Celtics.

    Moreover, Jarrett Culver secured his NBA playoffs berth for the first-time as the Grizzlies finished first in the Southwest division.

    But the team lost to Golden State Warriors in the Western Conference Semi-Finals by 2-4 in a series of six games.

    On September 12, 2022, he signed with Atlanta Hawks on a two-way contract. But the Hawks waived him on January 14, 2023, thus transferring his rights to the team’s NBA G-League affiliate College Park Skyhawks.

    Culver was traded from the College Park Skyhawks to the Rio Grande Valley Vipers on January 26, 2023.
