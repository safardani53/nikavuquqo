    William Denby Hanna is an American animator, voice actor, animator and musician. After working odd jobs during the early months of the Great Depression, Hanna joined Harman and Ising Animation Studio in 1930. During the 1930s, Hanna gradually gained a lot of skill and fame while working on animated films like Captain and the Kids. In 1937, while working at Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, Hanna met Joseph Barbera. The two began to collaborate first known for the production of Tom and Jerry. In 1957, they co-founded Hanna-Barbera, which became the most successful television animation studio in the industry, creating and / or producing shows like The Flintstones, The Huckleberry Hound Show, The Jetsons, Scooby-Doo. , The Smurfs, and the Yogi Bear. In 1967, Hanna-Barbera was sold to Taft Broadcasting for $ 12 million, but Hanna and Barbera remained in charge of the company until 1991.
