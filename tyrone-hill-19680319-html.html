    Tyrone Hill is a retired American basketball player and former assistant coach of the Atlanta Hawks of the National Basketball Association. Hill spent four years playing college at Xavier University, in his last season averaging 20.2 points and 12.6 rebounds per game, while shooting 58.1% from starting. The Golden State Warriors picked him with the 11th pick in the 1990 NBA Draft. After three years at Golden State, Hill was transferred to Cleveland Cavaliers in the summer of 1993. Playing under Mike Fratello, Hill won the All-Star Game in 1995. He set Cleveland's one-season franchise record by scoring a career record of 60.0 percent from the field. . Hill was sent to the Milwaukee Bucks on a three-team contract in 1997 that included Terrell Brandon and Shawn Kemp, and spent the rest of his career with the Philadelphia 76ers, Cleveland and the Miami Heat.
