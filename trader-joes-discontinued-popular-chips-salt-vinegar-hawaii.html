    FANS of Trader Joe's have criticized the grocery chain after a fan-favorite snack was axed from grocery shelves.

    The U.S. Sun confirmed with a Trader Joe's representative that the snack has been discontinued.

    "Trader Joe's, what have you done with your salt and vinegar chips," a fan tweeted.

    A Trader Joe's representative confirmed that the chips are no longer on shelves to The U.S. Sun.

    The chips were Hawaiian Styled salt and vinegar crisps - Hawaii is known for its high-quality sweet potatoes which are a common ingredient in gourmet crisps.

    On the bag, Trader Joe's said the chips were "cooked slowly in small batches using only fresh, unpeeled potatoes and expeller pressed vegetable oil."

    Read More on Trader Joe's

    Customers have had a strong reaction to the chips disappearance on Twitter.

    TWITTER ANGER

    Back when the chips still stocked grocery shelves, customers raved about the chips.

    "My gums and roof of my mouth are shredded beyond all recognition but I simply will not stop eating the trader joes salt and vinegar chips every single day," one person said back in April 2021.

    But the crisps were soon gone from all store shelves.

    Salt and vinegar fans went on tirades about the old chips.

    "The worst day of my f**kin life was the day Trader Joe's stopped carrying their salt and vinegar chips," one fan said.

    Another chimed in: "Still p**sed that trader joes discontinued the salt and vinegar chips."

    A third said: "you got rid of my salt and vinegar chips, and idk if I can forgive you."

    The chips join a list of Trader Joe's fan-favorites forever ripped from the hearts of hungry grocers.

    OTHER TJ'S DISCONTINUED ITEMS

    Shoppers at Trader Joe's are used to high-quality, well-discounted items.

    Because of TJ's unconventional sourcing, customers have grown used to discontinued items.

    Fans said goodbye to multiple fan favorites - including the Chocolate Hummus, Chunky Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough, and Creamy Cauliflower Jalapeno Dip.

    One Reddit thread called the cookie dough "the best retail cookie dough in America."
