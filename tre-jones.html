    Tre Jones | High School Career

    Jones attended Apple Valley High School and played basketball for the Varsity team in Apple Valley, Minnesota.

    He initially started playing in eighth grade and became a starter for the next season, ninth grade. He led his team to Minnesota 4A state titles two times in 2015 and 2017.

    Tre won the Gatorade Player of the Year award in Minnesota in 2016-17. His brother Tyus was also the winner of the same award in 2014.

    Tre managed to average 23.5 points, 10.4 rebounds, and 7.5 assists in the 2016–17 season. He also received McDonald’s All-American and Minnesota Mr. Basketball awards after his senior season.

    Tre was ranked No. 6 overall and the No. 1 rated point guard in 2018 by the 24/7 Composite list, and Scouts.com.ESPN ranked him as the No. 10 overall prospect and No. 1 point guard in the class of 2018.

    Similarly, Rivals.com ranked him as the No. 7 overall prospect and the No. 3 guard in the class of 2018.

    This was all after he chose to be the Duke player over UCLA, USC, Ohio State, Minnesota, and Oregon University.

    Tre Jones | College Career

    Freshman year (2018-2019)

    Jones started his college career with Duke on November 6, 2018. He led his team to take over Kentucky at the Champions Classic, averaging 6 points, 4 rebounds, and 7 assists in a  118–84 win.

    Jones again held his team to a 69–58 triumph over Texas Tech, chipping 13 points, 5 rebounds, 5 assists, and 6 steals.

    Unluckily, Jones was troubled with a shoulder injury on January 14, 2019, when he was put on a clash with Frank Howard of Syracuse. His situation got miserable, and he had to miss two games in the season.

    Jones made a comeback on January 26, when he was placed against  Georgia Tech. Overall, he scored  9.4 points for the freshman season, 5.3 assists, and 3.8 rebounds per game in 36 games for the Duke Blue Devils.

    Sophomore year (2019-2020)

    The Dukes declared Jones’s return for the 2019-20 season on April 8, 2019. He averaged 15 points in his first match of the sophomore season, a 68-66 win over Kansas.

    He proved himself again by notching 31 points in a 74-63 victory over Georgia State, which was also a highlighted part of his career. This was on November 15.

    Jones achieved 19 points on 9-of-13 shooting and grabbed six assists against Notre Dame (2/15).

    Not only that, he recorded 24 points at Wake Forest (2/25), shooting 7-of-18 from the floor and 9-of-10 from the foul line and grabbing nine assists and seven boards in 47 minutes.

    Jones led his team to win at Boston College (2/4) and North Carolina (2/8). He was honored with ACC Co-Player of the Week after leading Duke to win over at No. 11 Michigan State (12/3) and Virginia Tech (12/6), scoring 17.5 points and 8.0 assists.

    Jones made a powerful presence when he scored 31 points against Georgia State. And, he scored more than 30 points in Duke history.

    Luck did not favor him again, and Jones had to miss out on the games against Wofford and Brown. This was due to his not-so-severe foot injury in late December.

    On average, Jones scored 16.2 points, 6.4 assists, 4.2 rebounds, and 1.8 steals per game as a sophomore. As a consequence of his laudable playing style, he was named ACC Player of the Year and Defensive Player of the Year.

    Jones was titled second-team All-American by USA Today. Similarly, he was named third-team All-American by the Associated Press, Sports Illustrated, and CBS Sports.

    Moreover, it came out as no surprise when he got voted to the USBWA District III All-District Team. He stood a finalist for the Naismith Defensive Player of the Year.

    National Team Career

    He played at the  2015 FIBA Americas Under-16 Championship in Argentina for the United States, coming to his national team career. 

    And led his team to win the gold medal recording 19 steals in the competition. He was the second player to break the record of American under-16 after Malik Newman in 2013.
