    03 Apr 2024, 00:00

    MANCHESTER UNITED have included Richie Wellens' 16-year-old son Charlie in their Europa League training squad.

    The teenage midfielder signed a scholar contract with the Old Trafford club this summer after starring in their academy.

    He has been compared to Paul Scholes thanks to his eye for goal, with three strikes in five Under-18s starts this season.

    And now Wellens will have a chance to impress with the seniors as he trained alongside the likes of Harry Maguire and Marcus Rashford.

    Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has promised to use youth this season as United prepare to play Astana tomorrow night.

    SunSport exclusively revealed Mason Greenwood, 17, will get only his second start in a United shirt when they begin their Europa League campaign at home to Astana.

    The Norwegian boss said: “Mason's one of the best finishers I've seen and I've played with a few decent ones.

    “He's still very young but has matured in the last six months since I came in.

    “I want him him to go out there, enjoy himself, work hard and close down because when he has the ball at his feet I know he is going to score anyway and make the right finish."

    Latest Manchester United news

    Wellens' dad, who also came through the Red Devils' youth system, moved to Blackpool in 2000 after just one senior appearance for the club.

    He established himself at the Seasiders and went on to enjoy an 19-year career as a pro where he was best known for his time at Leicester.

    Wellens, 39, had a brief stint as Oldham boss but was axed after relegation to League Two before taking over Swindon last year.
