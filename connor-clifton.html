    Where is Connor Clifton from? Early Life and Family

    Connor Clifton was born on April 18, 1995, in Long Branch, New Jersey, United States. He was born to proud parents Tim Clifton Sr. and Joanie Clifton.

    Besides his parents, Connor has two brothers. He is the middle child in the family, while Tim Clifton Jr. is the eldest, and Corey Clifton is the youngest.

    Ice Hockey runs in the blood of the Clifton family. All three Clifton boys showed interest in it since childhood. In fact, all of them are doing great at ice hockey.

    They used to slide on a pair of skates, gliding across the frozen pond behind their home in Matawan, as kids. Their father was the one who founded the love for ice hockey in Clifton boys.

    Moreover, Tim, Connor, and Corey had the same beginning as all of them started their ice hockey venture from the Christians Brothers Academy. Likewise, all of them played for the New Jersey Hitmen, followed by Quinnipiac University.

    Connor Clifton and Tim Clifton got admitted for mechanical engineering at the University of Quinnipiac. The Clifton brothers share similar interests, but they have different playing styles.

    The eldest, Tim Clifton Jr., chose to stay in Matawan after high school graduation and flourish hockey in his hometown. He also played for the San Jose Barracuda of the American Hockey League (AHL).

    The youngest, Corey Clifton, initially has plans to follow in the footsteps of his brother Tim. The Clifton family bonds immensely when it comes to their adoration for ice hockey.

    Connor Clifton | Amateur Ice Hockey Career

    High School

    Connor Clifton studied at the Christians Brothers Academy. He was indulged in prep hockey while in school.

    While there, he played for the New Jersey Hitmen as a 14-year-old in 2009 in the former Empire Junior Hockey League (EJHL), the forerunner of today’s USPHL Elite Division.

    He then joined the New Jersey Hitmen’s top team in the former EJHL (forerunner of today’s NCDC). He scored 12 points in 28 games for the Hitmen as a 16-year-old.

    The Arizona Coyotes then drafted Clifton as the 133rd overall player in the fifth round of the NHL entry draft. However, they never signed him.

    Quinnipiac University

    The young New Jersey native then decided to develop his hockey skills in the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA).

    He then got admitted to Quinnipiac University, playing for the Quinnipiac Bobcats men’s ice hockey team for the next four years. He started his freshman season at Quinnipiac during the 2013-14 season.

    Clifton was a part of 156 games in association with the NCAA. In fact, he scored 56 points. He also demonstrated his offensive qualities, which is not the main strength of a right-handed defenseman.

    The New Jersey native was then named to the Eastern College Athletic Conference (ECAC) Hockey All-Academic Team for all four seasons with the Quinnipiac Bobcats.

    Clifton became the captain of Quinnipiac Bobcats while in junior year (2016-17). Also, he was named to the ECAC Hockey All-Tournament and NCAA East All-Frozen Four Team.

    The New Jersey native graduated from Quinnipiac as the school’s all-time penalty minutes leader with 284 in 156 games.

    He was on the NCAA All-Tournament selection when Quinnipiac reached the Frozen Four in 2016. However, they lost the National Championship game to the University of North Dakota.

    Further, Clifton received Tournament Most Valued Player (MVP) and All-Tournament honors from ECAC Hockey when the Bobcats won the conference title that year.
